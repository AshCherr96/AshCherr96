Biography

Early Life and Start in Theater

Vincent Leonard Price Jr. was born on May 27, 1911 in St. Louis, Missouri of the youngest of four children to Vincent Leonard Price Sr. and Marguerite Cobb Wilcox. Price's grandfather, Vincent Clarence Price, acquired the family fortune from his invention of the product "Dr. Price's Baking Powder" which was the first cream of tartar based baking powder. Price attended Yale University and graduated with a major in English and a minor in art history in 1933 and he also worked for the campus humor magazine titled, The Yale Record. He taught for a year before he began studying for his Master's degree in fine arts at Courtauld Institute of Art in London where he was drawn to the theater. He began acting on the stage professionally in 1934 and then moved on to work performing at Orson Welles's Mercury Theatre a year later. Price appeared in the American production of Laurence Housman's play, Victoria Regina, as Prince Albert in 1936 which he recieved great acclaim for his performance.

Work in Film

Vincent Price made his on screen debut as the male lead in romantic comedy film, Service de Luxe (1938). He appeared in more notable films in the following years that include Laura (1944) starring Gene Tierney; biographical film of Woodrow Wilson, Wilson (1944); The Song of Bernadette (1943); and The Keys of the Kingdom (1944) starring Gregory Peck. Price's first horror film would be in 1939's The Tower of London, but it would be twenty years before he would become established as a horror actor. In 1950, he played the lead role of James Reavis in the biopic, The Baron of Arizona. In 1953, he starred in the iconic horror film House of Wax which was the first 3D film to be in the top 10 at the box-office. He was then cast in more horror films in the following years that include The Mad Magician (1954); The Fly (1958); The Return of the Fly (1959); The Tingler (1959); the notable House on Haunted Hill (1959).

In the 1960s, Vincent Price collaborated on a series of horror films with director/producer Roger Corman for the production company, American International Pictures. The duo made seven films together and all of which were based on the literary works of Edgar Allen Poe:

  • House of Usher (1960)
  • The Pit and the Pendulum (1961)
  • Tales of Terror (1962)
  • The Haunted Palace (1963)
  • The Raven (1963)
  • The Tomb of Ligeia (1964)
  • The Masque of the Red Death (1964)

Fun fact! Vincent Price was actually a fan of Edgar Allen Poe's works and visited the Edgar Allen Poe Museum in Richmond, Virginia where he had his photo taken with the famous stuffed raven!

After his series of successful horror films in the 1960s, Price became a well established and renowned horror actor being named among the horror legends of Boris Karloff, Bela Lugosi, and Lon Chaney. During the 1970s, Price's horror career continued as he played the lead in films The Abominable Dr. Phibes (1971), Dr. Phibes Rises Again (1972), and Theatre of Blood (1973).

TV and Other Appearances

Vincent made numerous appearances in tv shows beginning in the 1950s and through the 1980s. Here's just a few of the tv shows he appeared on:

  • Playhouse 90
  • Hollywood Squares
  • Get Smart
  • Batman
  • The Man From U.N.C.L.E.
  • The 13 Ghosts of Scooby-Doo

Vincent was also active in radio and made appearances in several serials, and he also made audio recordings of the works of Edgar Allen Poe.

Family Life

Vincent married three times to Edith Barrett (1938-1948); Mary Grant (1949-1973); and Coral Browne (1974-1991). He has a son, Vincent Barrett Price, born in 1940 with wife Edith Barrett and a daughter, Mary Victoria Price, born in 1962 with wife Mary Grant.

Later Life

Vincent continued doing steady work into the 1980s as he was the narrator for Tim Burton's short film, Vincent, and had a spoken-word part throughout Michael Jackson's hit song, Thriller. He appeared in film, House of the Long Shadows (1983), along with Christopher Lee, Peter Cushing, and John Carridine. In 1986 he did the voice of Professor Ratigan in Disney film, The Great Mouse Detective, which he stated was one of his favorite roles to perform. He received his only major award nomination during his career when he was nominated for Best Supporting Male Actor at the Independent Spirit Awards for film, The Whales of August (1987), which also starred actresses Bette Davis and Lillian Gish. In 1989, Price was inducted into the St. Louis Walk of Fame and he appeared in his last film in 1990, Tim Burton's Edward Scissorhands alongside Johnny Depp. During the end of his life, he suffered from COPD and Parkinson's disease and at the age of 82 he passed away from lung cancer on October 25, 1993 at his home in Los Angeles.

His Legacy!

Vincent Price remains one of the most revered actors to have ever lived, especially in the horror genre! He and his body of work are still referenced throughout the entertainment industry to this day! He has made lasting impacts in not only the film industry but also in the art and culinary world (go to the Published Works page! and Art pages! to learn more!). He was a unique gem to the world for his talent as well as a generous, giving heart and has been an inspiration to many and will continue to be for years to come.
