Big Cypress Tree State Park

Big Cypress Tree State Park is located in Greenfield, TN and is a beautiful, peaceful spot with various plant life and wildlife to experience as well as fun activities to take part in.

history

Big Cypress Tree State Park is named after the national champion bald cypress that was once in the park. The tree was the largest tree species west of the Mississippi River, but in 1976 the tree was struck by lightening and died after living for 1,350 years.

What the Park Offers

  • Educational Programs that include field trips and custom programs.
  • Hiking on tree identification trail and a boardwalk trail.
  • The park is popular for those who partake in birdwatching.
  • Various wildlife and plant life.
  • Individual camping and camping for boy scout troops

Nearby Attractions

There are a couple of nearby attractions to visit that include Davy Crockett's last home place and the Humboldt Strawberry Festival Historical Museum.

Annual Events

  • May: West TN Strawberry Festival
  • Sept: TN Soybean Festival
  • Oct: Greenfield Fire Prevention Festival
  • Oct: Fall Folklore Jamboree
