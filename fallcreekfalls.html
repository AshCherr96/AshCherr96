Fall Creek Falls

Fall Creek Falls State Park is located in the eastern portion of the Cumberland Plateau. This state park covers over 29,000 acres and includes multiple waterfalls.

history

The federal government initially purchased this land in 1937. The Works Progress Administration and Civilian Conservation Corps began constructing facilities and restoring the forest in 1938. Ownership was transferred to Tennesee in 1944. history!

things to do

  • Visit the waterfalls (Fall Creek Falls, Cane Creek Falls, Cane Creek Cascades, Rockhouse Falls, Piney Creek Falls, Coon Creek Falls, and Lost Creek Falls)
  • Visit the overlooks.
  • Go caving (Rumbling Falls Cave, Camps Gulf Cave, Lost Creek Cave
  • Wildlife watch
  • Play Golf
  • Go Hiking (56+ miles of trails)
  • Mountain Biking (24 miles)
  • Go Rock Climbing
  • Enjoy the Canopy Challenge Course
  • Go boating.

places to stay

Enjoy a visit by staying at the lodge (currently being renovated), one of the group lodges, the campground, the group campground, or one of our cabins (check out the fisherman cabins that sit on the lake). If our facilities are full, please consider staying at a cabin at nearby Cumberland Mountain State Park.
